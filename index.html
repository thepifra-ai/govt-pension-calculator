Pension Calculation Formula in Pakistan

The Government of Pakistan uses a standard formula to calculate pension.

Gross Pension = (Total Emoluments × Qualifying Service × 7) / 300

  • Total emoluments include basic pay and pensionable allowances
  • Qualifying service is your total service years up to a maximum of 30
  • The ratio 7/300 is fixed by the government

This calculator applies the formula automatically, so you do not need to do any manual calculations.

What is Retiring Year Increment?

The retiring year increment is one of the most important factors in pension calculation, but many employees are not aware of it.

If you retire between July and November, you are eligible for one extra annual increment. This increases your final basic pay and ultimately your pension amount. If you retire in other months, this increment is not applied.

This pension calculator automatically checks your retirement date and applies this rule correctly, so you do not miss out on your actual pension value.

Types of Pension in Pakistan

Pension types vary depending on the situation of the employee. Understanding the correct type is important for accurate calculation.

  • Superannuation pension for retirement at age 60
  • Retiring pension for early retirement
  • Death during service pension for family support
  • Death after retirement pension for dependents

Commutation of Pension Explained

Commutation allows a government employee to receive a portion of pension as a lump sum at the time of retirement. This reduces the monthly pension for a specific period.

  • Maximum commutation allowed is 35 percent
  • Monthly pension reduces after commutation
  • Full pension is restored after 12 to 15 years

Many employees choose commutation without understanding its long term impact. This calculator clearly shows both the lump sum and reduced pension so you can make a better decision.
